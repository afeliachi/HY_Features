The HY_WaterBody feature type specializes the general HY_HydroFeature class. The water body is part of the hydrographic network, either standing in a water pool, or flowing as stream in a watercourse, which are parts of the channel network. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>A water body associates a <i>fixed landmark</i> which could be used to describe a permanent reference location on the water body which realizes the conceptual nexus of the catchment that is realized by that water body. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>A water body may be segmented in vertical sections at right angles to the main (average) direction of flow or along its centerline, and horizontal strata. Conceptually, each water body, or a stratum, is understood as a reservoir used for storage, regulation or control of water recourses. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p><br /></p><p>HY_WaterBody inherits from generalization the <i>name</i> property and the <i>flow-line</i> constraints. It associates the <i>hydrographicNetwork</i> in which it participates, the containing <i>waterpool</i> and <i>watercourse</i>, the vertical <i>streamCrossSection</i>, <i>streamLongitudinalSection</i>, the <i>fixedLandmark</i>,  the horizontal <i>stratum</i>, and the reservoir for the <i>storage</i> of water. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>An o<i>utlet-at-section</i> and <i>outlet-at-landmark</i> constraint emphasizes the recognition of vertical section as (said to be coincident with) the outlet of the catchment that is realized by the hydrographic network the station is part of.<br /></p>
