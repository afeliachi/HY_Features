The River Positioning System (Figure 37) provides a simple model to place a feature of interest 'on a river'. It introduces the concept of indirect position where a position is determined relative to an already located referent, usually the outlet of a catchment, or a feature recognized as (said to be coincident with) thh outlet.<br /></p><p>This concept uses a linear river referencing using the flowpath realizing a particular catchment as linear element along which as position can be assigned to a feature of interest relative to the catchment's outlet located on that flowpath. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>The River Referencing references the one-dimensional topological realization of catchment within the implied hydrologic complex in such that the already located referent may be recognized as a node on the boundary of the flowpath edge. Given that a flowpath realizes a particular catchment between inflow and outflow, the catchment outlet realizing that catchment forms the known referent located either at the upstream or the downstream end of the flowpath. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>A feature of interest then can be located along the flowpath referencing the located catchment  outlet, or a feature that is recognized as (said to be coincident with) the outlet (Figures 18 and Figure 19).  The position of is expressed/measured as distance from that catchment outlet. Using located referents in both directions allows placement of a feature of interest between both referents and expression of the intermediate position as percentage of the measured distance (Figure 20).<br /></p>
