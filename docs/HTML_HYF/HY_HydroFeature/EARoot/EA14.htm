The Hydro Complex package is a collection of hydrologic features that form a hydrologically closed system. This includes the catchment, its inflow and outflow (conceptualized as hydrologic nexus) as well as various realizations of a catchment by typical hydrologic features. In this complex, the hydrologically determined unity of catchment and its common outlet forms an internal catchment (topology) pattern recognizing the catchment as a link between inflow and outflow, expressed for example as a flowpath 'bounded' by the catchment outlet at upstream or downstream end. This catchment pattern is then applied to describe the connectivity of catchments in hydrographic, channel networks, or hydrometric networks. The Hydro Complex model also allows for catchments to be recognized through reference to a catchment's outlet even if stream networks, catchment areas or catchment boundary are not available. It is intended that hydrological reporting applications may use this model without the full complexity and detail of scientific catchment models.<br /></p><p><br /></p><p>The <b>Catchment</b> model conceptualizes the internal catchment pattern through inter-related 'catchment realization' features such as catchment outlet and flowpath , and through an 'hydroNexus' feature in the role of the connector between interacting catchments  (Figure 26 and Figure 27) whereby each catchment realizes the internal catchment pattern. Conceptually, each catchment has a nexus, and any nexus has a corresponding catchment, even if catchment and/or nexus may not be present in a particular application. Catchments interact with upper and lower catchments via the associated nexus, and ultimately contribute to the common outlet  of a containing catchment. <br /></p>
