The informative Atmospheric Hydro Feature package provides a model to take into account water from atmospheric origin, particularly from precipitation, without the complexity and detail of a rainfall-runoff model.  This enables contextually related information models to build relationships to hydrologic features, finally to a represented catchment. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>Conceptionally, water falling through the atmosphere (precipitation) is understood to be accumulated in water bodies on and below the land surface. This allows precipitation to be related to the catchment represented by the hydrographic network of water bodies.<br /></p><p><br /></p><p>Special concepts of water suspended in the atmosphere may by defined by application. These concepts should not contradict the definitions endorsed by the WMO as published in the  "International Meteorological Vocabulary".<br /></p>
