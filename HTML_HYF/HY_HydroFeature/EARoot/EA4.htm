The hydrographic network model denotes the network of permanent and temporary water bodies connected using a connecting channel network. Depending on the application, the channel network and the related features may be described by suitable attributes.<br /></p><p><br /></p><p>The hydrographic network is defined independent of the channel network. This conceptual separation references the specific concerns of hydrology studying the occurrence, accumulation, and circulation of water. This approach allows to realise the logical catchment as a network of flowing or stagnant water bodies, regardless of the channel wherein it may move, or not. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>If required, an application dealing with surface water bodies make use of the concepts when describing the relationship to the channel network, or to the catchment realised by the hydrographic network.<br /></p><p><br /></p><p>Note: The definitions in this package are  rooted in the definition given in the WMO Glossary of Hydrology which defines the hydrographic network, water body, and stratum regardless of the location in the respect to the Earth's surface. For the purpose of testing the applicability of the conceptual model in the context of surface water hydrology, in this standard 'hydrographic network' and its parts refer to the water accumulated in surface water bodies on the land surface. <br /></p>
