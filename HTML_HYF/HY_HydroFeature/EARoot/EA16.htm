AtmosphericHydrofeature  and SubsurfaceHydrofeature informative packages are intentionally provided to relate water from atmosphere (rain and snow) reaching the land surface as well as subsurface water (groundwater and vadose water) accumulated under ground to the hydrographic network and finally the represented catchment. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>Provided  a relationship to HY_WaterLiquidPhase / HY_WaterSolidPhase classes is defined, different types of atmospheric or a subsubsurface  water may be related to the accumulating water body (segment), part of the hydrographic network associated with a reference point located in the logical network of catchments.<br /></p><p><br /></p><p>Provided a relationship to the HY_ReferencePoint is defined, atmospheric and subsurface hydro features located in the network of catchments may get assigned a position relative to an identified reference point.<br /></p><p><br /></p><p>Special concepts of water suspended in the atmosphere, or of water accumulated below the land surface, may by defined. Such concepts should not contradict the definitions endorsed by the WMO, as published in the  "International Glossary of Hydrology" or "International Meteorological Vocabulary".<br /></p>
