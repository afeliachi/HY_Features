This means that no catchment exists without a corresponding outfall even if this outfall may have a zero value within an application.<br /></p><p><br /></p><p>Note: The holistic catchment approach refers to the common recognition of a catchment as the basic unit of study in hydrology, in water resources management and environmental reporting. It refers to the continuous interaction of surface and subsurface waters within a catchment, even if a particular representation refers only to one or the other. This definition is rooted in the definitions given in the WMO Glossary of Hydrology,  regardless of the location in the respect to the Earth's surface. For the purpose of testing the applicability of the conceptual model in the context of surface water hydrology, in this standard the definition of 'catchment' refers to a unit having a common outlet for its surface runoff.<br /></p>
