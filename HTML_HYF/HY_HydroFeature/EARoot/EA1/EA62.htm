feature realisation of the conceptual outfall, specifically as a permanent <font color="#231f20">and clearly identified </font>reference location placed at the outfall it realises, incl. a single point. the reference point may be represented geometrically as a point identified by coordinates. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>Note: This definition defines a permanent, stable reference location fixed and referenced by coordinates, regardless of their location in the respect to the Earth's surface. For the purpose of testing the applicability of the conceptual model in the context of surface water hydrology, the definition of 'reference location' in this standard refers to a location associated with a surface channel or surface water body. <br /></p>
