The <b>HY_Catchment</b> feature type captures the hydrological determination of a catchment by its common outlet (Figure 27-34) and the multiple realization of this dependency (Figure 13 and 35) in typical catchment-realization features including their possible geometric representation. Using these  features the catchment is topologically described as set of internal hydrologic relationships that connect flows from atmospheric and groundwater sources to the catchment's outlet.  The HY_Catchment associates the hydrological nexus whereto the catchment contributes outflow or from which it receives inflow.  <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>Catchments may be organized in a simple  containment hierarchy as typically used for management and reporting units. Catchments may interact with upper and lower catchments immediately above or below, or in terms of a conjoint catchment  across an internal boundary such as diffuse divide between non-delineated sub-catchments.<br /></p><p><br /></p><p>The <b>HY_Catchment</b> feature type specializes the general HY_HydroFeature type. Through generalization, HY_Catchment inherits the <i>name</i> property. It carries a <i>code</i> attribute and the associations: <i>outflow</i>, <i>inflow</i>, <i>containingCatchment</i>, <i>containedCatchment</i>, <i>conjointCatchment</i>, <i>upperCatchment</i>, <i>lowerCatchment</i>,and <i>catchmentRealizationFeature</i>.<br /></p>
