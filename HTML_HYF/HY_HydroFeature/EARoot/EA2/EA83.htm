The <b>HY_CatchmentRealization</b> feature type (Figure 35) conceptualizes the multiple realization of an 'un-realized' logical catchment by typical features used to describe the internal, hydrologically determined relationships connecting flows from atmospheric and groundwater sources to the catchment's common outlet. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>Each catchment-realization feature may be connected with IN-coming or OUT-going features of the same type to assist a topology-based sorting of these features in typical network. For example, a sorting by incoming upward flowpaths may assist the navigation from mouth to source.<br /></p><p><br /></p><p>The catchment-realization concept implies a hydrologic complex in that, if a catchment realization exists, this exists in the same hydrologic complex as the catchment it realizes. Any feature realization of the logical catchment references the hydrologic determination of the realized catchment. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>The catchment realization features defined in this standard refer to objects on the land surface for the purpose of surface water hydrology. In other contexts, other types of catchment realization may exist. Catchment realizations that do not conform to those defined in this standard, for instance realizations in 3- or 4-dimensional perspectives, may be implemented specializing/using the general HY_CatchmentRealization type. HY_CatchmentRealization carries a <i>shape</i> attrtibute, and <i>realizedCatchment</i>,<i> IN(coming)</i> and <i>OUT(going)</i> associations.<br /></p>
