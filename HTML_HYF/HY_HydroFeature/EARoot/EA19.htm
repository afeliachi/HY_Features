DRAFT: <br /></p><p>The HY_HydroComplex package defines the union between catchment and its common outlet, the relationships between catchments  as well as the multiple realisation of catchments. The catchment model conceptualize the ‘idea’ of a catchment wherein water occurs between inflow and outflow locations understood as boundaries of the catchment inside.  It refers to the common, holistic approach of a catchment drained using a connecting system of drainage channels. The catchment realization model defines the realizations of the conceptual catchment most common in the hydrology domain and the relationships between them. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>The catchmentmodel defines an hierarchical network of catchments that may be understood as an aggregate of catchments within an ultimate containing catchment, whose inflow and outflow forms the boundary for all encapsulated parts. Each catchment is defined as an indivisible union (topological closure) of the catchment and its outfall in the role as inflow or outflow of the corresponding catchment, or both. This means that there exists no outfall without a catchment, and no catchment without outfall, even if it may have a zero value. The outfall of the catchment is always of lower topological dimension than the catchment inside, e.g. a catchment understood as a topological edge is 'bounded' by inflow and/or outflow nodes, a catchment understood a face by summit line and/or flowline edges.<br /></p><p><br /></p><p>Each catchment may be multiple realized in any hydrologically meaningful sub-domain model. The catchment model defines most common types such as flowpath, catchment area and catchment boundary as the  basic realizations of the catchment topology as well as  a network realizing the catchment network.<br /></p>
