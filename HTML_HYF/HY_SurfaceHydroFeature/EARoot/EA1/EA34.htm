feature realisation of an 'un-realised' catchment specifically as a path connecting the inflow and outflow of the logical catchment it realises, incl. a straight line.  a flowpath realises the catchment topologically in terms of an edge connecting two outfalls realised as nodes, and may be represented geometrically as a curve. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>The flowpath concept implies a hydrologic complex as a collection of related hydrologic features closed under the hydrologic determination condition in such that if a flowpath exists, this flowpath references the hydrologic determination of the catchment it realises. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>Note: This definition is rooted in the definition given in the WMO Glossary of hydrology which defines a path line as the curve described by a moving particle of water, regardless of water occurs above or below the Earth's surface. For the purpose of testing the applicability of the conceptual model in the context of surface water hydrology, the definition of 'flow path' in this standard refers to a moving particle of surface water.<br /></p>
