feature that realises the logical outfall within a hydrologic complex, particularly as a permanent, stable reference location fixed and referenced by coordinates. any geometric object, incl. a point may represent the reference location.<br /></p><p><br /></p><p>the outfall realisation concept implies a hydrologic complex as a collection of related hydrologic features closed under the 'catchment' determination in such that if a outfall realisation exists, these features are in the same hydrologic complex as the outfall they realise. If the realised outfall is topologically connected (i.e. if its corresponding catchment is explicitly not null) then the hydrologic features in the outfall realisation are also connected.<br /></p><p><br /></p><p>Note: This definition defines a permanent, stable reference location  regardless of their location in the respect to the Earth's surface. For the purpose of testing the applicability of the conceptual model in the context of surface water hydrology, the definition of 'reference location' in this standard refers to a location associated with a surface channel or surface water body.<br /></p>
