The HY_HydroComplex leaf package defines a logical network of catchments each multiple realised  in any hydrologically meaningful sub-domain model, including by a hydrographic network. It defines the relationship between  a catchment and its common outlet, and the relationships between catchments. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>The HY_HydroComplex leaf a provides a collection of hydrologic features closed under the condition of the hydrologic determination of the catchment.   This means that each specialisation and any realisation of  the catchment will reference the hydrologic determination.<br /></p><p><br /></p><p>The catchment model allows for the existence of catchments to be recognized, and identifiers assigned referencing the common outlet even if stream networks, areas and watersheds are not reliably determined. This is sufficient to define simplified hierarchies. It is intended that hydrological reporting applications may use this model without the full complexity and detail of scientific catchment models. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>The catchment model provides a concept to consider an arbitrary location on, or projected onto, the land surface as the outfall of a corresponding catchment. Positioned in the logical network of catchments, such a real' outfall provides an identifiable reference to which alternative catchment  realisations may refer.  If a realised catchment is connected with other catchments, its realisations are also connected.  The logical network of catchments allows to establish topological relationships between hydrographic, hydrometric features and other realisations without the detail of stream flow, flow conditions or cartographic interpretation. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p><br /></p><p><br /></p><p><br /></p>
